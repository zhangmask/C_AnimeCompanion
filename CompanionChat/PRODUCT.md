# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Anime Companion is for people who want a private, persistent AI companion on their phone, with a future path toward a wearable companion device. Users are in intimate, repeat-use mobile contexts: chatting, speaking hands-free, managing memories, choosing character identities, and shaping a companion that feels continuous over time.

## Product Purpose

The product makes local AI companionship feel owned, remembered, and expressive. It combines on-device chat, role cards, long-term memory, preference learning, voice interaction, and image capabilities so the companion behaves less like a disposable assistant and more like a familiar character.

Success means the user can quickly enter conversation, switch or create a role, trust memory and privacy boundaries, and understand which local or network capabilities are active without reading documentation inside the app.

## Brand Personality

Private, intimate, capable. The interface should feel calm enough for repeated daily use, emotionally warm enough for companionship, and technically credible enough for local model and device workflows.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS landing-page composition, decorative card overload, cloud-assistant stiffness, casino-like monetization patterns, and noisy marketplace screens that make character discovery feel transactional before it feels personal.

## Design Principles

- Keep companionship first: role, memory, and voice should support the feeling of a stable companion, not a settings-heavy tool.
- Make local ownership visible: clearly distinguish local, HTTP, unavailable, and fallback states.
- Let dense workflows stay calm: discovery, role management, and generation controls should be scannable on mobile without becoming promotional.
- Preserve the chat path: creation, collection, image generation, and voice setup must never block starting a conversation.
- Treat adult/private boundaries as user-controlled product state, not marketing decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target accessible Material 3 defaults, readable contrast in light and dark themes, touch targets suitable for one-handed mobile use, reduced reliance on color-only state, and restrained motion that communicates state rather than decoration.
